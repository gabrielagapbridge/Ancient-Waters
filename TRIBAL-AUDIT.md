# Ancient Waters — Complete Tribal & Cultural Inventory

## Purpose
Full audit of every tribe/culture with data in the project, organized by culture with all associated layers/features listed. For use in redesigning the UI from layer-based to culture-based organization.

---

## 1. CHUMASH (Hokan family)
**Time period:** 13,000+ years BP to contact (~1769–1834 missionization)
**Territory:** Santa Barbara coast, Ventura coast, Channel Islands, interior valleys (San Emigdio, Cuyama), south to Malibu

**8 linguistic divisions mapped:**
- Obispeño, Purisimeño, Ineseño/Samala, Barbareño, Ventureño, Emigdiano, Cuyama, Island (Isleño)

### Layers with Chumash content:
| Layer file | Content | Feature count |
|---|---|---|
| `21-chumash-rockart.js` | 10 rock art sites (Sapaksi/Painted Rock, Burro Flats, Pool Rock, Bear Track Cave, Condor Cave, Three Springs, House of Two Suns, Indian Caves, Mutau Flat, Tule River); 4 sacred landscape markers; 8 territorial division polygons; 5 Spanish missions | ~27 features |
| `22-chumash-waterways.js` | 10 rivers/creeks (Santa Ynez, Cuyama, Sisquoc, Santa Maria, Ventura, Santa Clara, Malibu Creek, Sespe, Piru, Zanja de Cota); 9 dams; 8 sacred water sites (Montgomery Springs, Malibu Lagoon, SB Channel Heritage NMS, etc.) | ~27 features |
| `23-chumash-villages.js` | 45+ village sites organized by linguistic division; surviving placenames marker (17 words) | ~47 features |
| `24-lost-chumash-waters.js` | 16 lost/degraded water sites (Mescaltitlan Island, Goleta Bay, Devereux, Carpinteria Marsh, etc.); 3 ghost polygons; statistics marker | ~20 features |
| `25-chumash-trade-network.js` | 5 tomol sea routes; 3 obsidian trade routes; 13 resource/trade nodes (shell bead mint, obsidian sources, asphaltum, chert, trade hubs); shell bead range marker | ~22 features |
| `18-southcentral-rockart.js` | 6 sites attributed to Chumash or Chumash/Yokuts shared | 6 sites |
| `19-tribal-encyclopedia.js` | "Chumash" and "Chumash Rock Art" encyclopedia entries | 2 entries |

**TOTAL: ~151 features — by far the most developed culture in the project**

## PRE-CULTURAL / CROSS-CULTURAL LAYERS

### PCN Rock Art Tradition (3,000–8,000 BP)
Pre-dates all known tribal boundaries. Found across Coast Range from Oregon to Santa Barbara.
- `06-rockart-pcn.js`: 6+ PCN sites (Ring Mountain, Canyon Trail Park, Alvarado Park, San Marin, etc.)
- Also appears in South-Central rock art layer

### Pleistocene / Paleo-Indian
- `03-water-features.js`: Ancient pluvial lakes (Lake Cahuilla, Lake Estancia, Lake San Agustín, Lake Lucero/Otero)
- `10-greatbasin.js`: Winnemucca Lake petroglyphs (10,500–14,800 BP — oldest in North America)
- `19-tribal-encyclopedia.js`: "Pleistocene Great Basin", "Lovelock Culture", "Lake Corcoran", "Lahontan Remnants"

### Spanish Colonial (cross-cultural)
- `04-southwest-sites.js`: 6 Spanish colonial sites (San Xavier del Bac, Santa Fe, Taos encounter, etc.)
- `21-chumash-rockart.js`: 5 Chumash-area missions
- `08-bayarea-shellmounds.js`: Fort Ross (Russian/Kashaya/Coast Miwok)

**Cultures with enough data for their own section (5+ layers or 20+ features):**
- Chumash, Coast Miwok, Bay Miwok/Volvon, Ohlone, Martis Complex

**Cultures that could be grouped regionally:**
- "Southwest" → Ancestral Puebloans + Hohokam + Sinagua + Pueblo peoples + Havasupai
- "Great Basin" → Northern Paiute + Western Shoshone + Southern Paiute + Washoe + Mohave
- "South-Central California" → Yokuts + Kawaiisu + Tataviam + Kitanemuk + Tübatulabal
- "North Bay" → Coast Miwok + Kashaya Pomo + Patwin + Wappo (+ Southern Pomo)
- "East Bay" → Bay Miwok/Volvon + Ohlone
- "Channel Coast" → Chumash (all subdivisions)

**Cross-cultural layers that need special handling:**
- PCN rock art (predates all cultures)
- Pleistocene/paleo features
- Spanish colonial encounters
- Bay fill / construction discoveries
- Language & tribal encyclopedias
