Handkerchief Rock — Tazewell County, VA
Started: March 31, 2026
Active
Lozenge-shaped group of red and black squares on vertical cliff, 5 mi E of Paint Lick, 7 mi W of Tazewell. Described by Mallery 1893. No modern documentation found. Estimated coordinates: 37.08, -81.59 (±2.5 mi).
Sources: Mallery 1893 pp. 121–122; Coale 1878 Ch. XXIII; Hranicky 1987 (not yet located)

Research Notes

[3/31/2026]
Mallery: "five miles east...7 west of Tazewell, high up against a vertical cliff." Lozenge-shaped red and black squares. Same ridge as Paint Lick. Only visible from across valley. Description cuts off mid-sentence.
[3/31/2026]
1902 USPS application for Akers PO confirms Knob PO was ~9mi from Tazewell, matching Mallery. Handkerchief Rock position places it in Akers/Thompson Valley area.
[3/31/2026]
Paint Lick is an entire ridge, not single peak. GNIS shows 2 summits 4mi apart. Handkerchief Rock is somewhere along this extended ridge.
[3/31/2026]
Indian Creek runs near Paint Lick base. 1915 USPS Bandy PO form confirms. Creek name likely reflects Indigenous presence.
[3/31/2026]
Next: Locate Hranicky 1987; check USGS old topos for Knob locality; GNIS search Indian Creek Tazewell; plan field visit with binoculars from across Thompson Valley.
Cherokee-Shawnee Battlefield & Buffalo Lick — Tazewell County, VA
Started: March 31, 2026
Active
Battle between Cherokee and Shawnee 1740–1750 over buffalo lick on mountain neighboring Paint Lick. Fortification remains visible in 1871. No modern documentation found. Estimated coordinates: 37.07, -81.65 (±5 mi).
Sources: Coale 1878 Ch. XXIII (via Mallery 1893)

Research Notes

[3/31/2026]
Coale links pictographs to territorial conflict. Buffalo lick = major resource node. Fortifications suggest substantial engagement.
[3/31/2026]
